01 / About

Background

I am a Lecturer in the Cyber Security Science Department at the Federal University of Technology, Minna, where I teach and guide research on human-centered security solutions. My work focuses on critical areas like Privacy and Security, Digital Forensics, and Human Factors in Cybersecurity, alongside a growing emphasis on Artificial Intelligence.

My research aims to enhance cybersecurity awareness and develop culturally relevant, user-centered training tools — particularly for Nigerian contexts. I have led impactful projects, including ScamNada, a dynamic anti-scam framework for digital banking users, and a ransomware awareness game designed to improve user resilience to cyber threats.

My research has been published in international journals and conferences, with applications spanning mobile banking security, digital forensics, and critical infrastructure protection in Nigeria.

ScamNada Framework

A dynamic anti-scam framework and gamified awareness approach for digital banking users in Nigeria. Researches scam techniques and builds educational tools for user-centric scam prevention.

  • Developed framework knowledge base for scam prevention
  • Supervised undergraduate FYP implementation

Ransomware Active

Ransomware Awareness Game

An interactive mobile game that simulates real-world ransomware incidents to prepare users with practical decision-making skills against evolving ransomware threats.

  • Conceptualized and supervised game development
  • Integrated cybersecurity principles with game mechanics
  • Reflects up-to-date ransomware threat landscape
Jan 2024 – Present
Digital Policing Completed

Digital Policing — NPF

Reviewed and evaluated 18 police-assisted mobile applications to assess forensic soundness and proposed a forensic-process model for the Nigerian Police Force.

  • Evaluated 6 Nigerian police-assisted apps
  • Proposed forensic-process model for NPF
  • Developed mathematical model for digital evidence admissibility
Jan – Dec 2018
Mobile Forensics Completed

Forensic Analysis of Mobile Banking Apps

Conducted forensic analysis on mobile banking applications of top Nigerian banks in partnership with NITDA, uncovering significant user data retention vulnerabilities.

  • Analyzed 12 mobile banking apps — all had vulnerabilities
  • Used UFED Touch & FRED for internal memory acquisition
  • Partnered with NITDA forensic laboratory
